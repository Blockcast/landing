Our Vision

To eliminate bandwidth scarcity and make rich interactive media accessible to EVERYONE from EVERYWHERE

Most of the world budgets their Internet traffic because their usage is tied to their bill. If you've waited to connect to Wi-Fi to download a video, you fit in this list. What if your network knew which applications you use and loaded your favorite content before you actually needed them? By building new networks with more delivery methods and caching options that application developers can leverage, we can utilize network resources more efficiently, and pass on savings to our users, and provide a better browsing experience.

Billions of people of people are coming online with inexpensive devices. Users are not able to make the full use of them because apps today are expensive to use because they are hungrier than ever before for data. Network capacity has not scaled in the same way or uniformly in geography in the same way devices have. Our internet infrastructure is aging and we need to take this as an opportunity to design networks that can scale this growth, bring applications closer to their customers, and give users more control of their bill.

Users pay a flat monthly fee and unlimited data is the default with Blockcast. We are strong supporters of an open and neutral internet and we don't limit or restrict particular applications. Application developers can optimize the delivery of their content by serving it from the secure modem cache, which can be update effeciently with multicasting.

More effecient

Secure local cache

Trusted applications can leverage a secure local proxy to terminate SSL and a webserver to serve cached materials. This can drastically reduce network congestion and reduce loading times. Because the satellite is a broadcast medium, applications can multicast streams off peak hours to fill caches, which users can enjoy later. Data is encrypted and keys released to the intended audience.

How it works

Traffic flow from client to Blockcast box to VPN proxy and response via satellite.

Asymmetric link acceess

Our satellite fleet operates in the Ku band and can be received with a standard 1.2m dish used for digital television. A built in DVB-S2/S2X demodulator chip zaps to the channel creating a shared data link that can burst up to 216 MBit/s in clear conditions. Unicast traffic is IPsec by default. Relevant traffic is handled at the local gateway in a bridge to a Wi-Fi or a physical interface. Response traffic back to the satellite gateway is tunneled through a bonded interface in active backup between OpenVPN L2TP and a GRE tunnel over the available uplink.
Clients are issued 802.1x credentials that they can setup their box in hotspot mode for a public space or as a private LAN for home. Once authenticated with our server, users can issue network requests that can either be routed locally or to remote servers. With over 90% of user traffic being downloads, this solution can convert a low throughput terrestrial connection into an uplink to introduce broadband service in areas with restricted or limited connectivity.

Open source Access Point

The access point is an open source edge platform. It has managed software switch which can receive configuration updates from a network controller. It also has libraries which allow developers to leverage addtional services such as secure storage and ceritficate management for SSL termination. Developers can also schedule content delivery over broadcast and multicast messages to cost effectively deliver or zero rate cacheable content such as the bitcoin blockchain or popular videos.
